Child rights protection

Rwanda has also made a strong commitment to the promotion of the Rights of Children and their protection by ratifying the International Conventions and Protocols on Children’s Rights, and through the establishment of the National Commission for the Child, as well as the Child Right’s Observatory YiDHO work daily to day to give children a healthy start in life, the opportunity to learn and protection from harm through child rights promotion and advocacy

Teenage pregnancies prevention

In Rwanda, teenage pregnancy among teenage girls indicates an increase of 6.1% to 7.3% through demographic and health survey (DHS) 2014-2015.in 2016, 17500 girls among 16-19 age group got premature unwanted pregnancy and it was taken as social epidemic. YiDHO took initiative of fighting against teenage pregnancy through approaching, educating and advising youth as the way to provide trusted information related to sexual reproductive health and rights and also train youth about family planning and teenage pregnancy preventions

HIV/AIDS prevention

In Rwanda, HIV prevalence has been stable since 2005 and remains at 3% among adults age 15–49 (4% among women and 2% among men). The prevalence of HIV is higher in urban areas (6%) than rural areas (2%); HIV prevalence is 6% in the capital city of Kigali and 2–3% in each of the other provinces.YiDHO will in place of Sensitizing HIV/AIDS testing and Condoms distribution for female sex workers with counselling. And also by making awareness campaign in the district of interest.
